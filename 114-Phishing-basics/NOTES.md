# Notes – Phishing Basics

---

## 🔹 What is Phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering attack used to trick users into:
- Revealing credentials
- Downloading malware
- Clicking malicious links

---

## 🔹 Why Phishing Works

- Targets human psychology
- Bypasses technical security controls
- Exploits trust and urgency

---

## 🔹 Psychology Behind Phishing

Common triggers:
- Urgency ("Your account will be locked")
- Authority ("Admin request")
- Curiosity
- Fear

---

## 🔹 Common Phishing Techniques

- Email phishing
- Spear phishing
- Clone phishing
- Whaling (targeting executives)

---

## 🔹 Phishing Campaign Structure

1. Reconnaissance
2. Crafting the message
3. Delivery
4. Exploitation (click/credential entry)
5. Post-exploitation

---

## 🔹 Social Engineering Toolkit (SET)

- Used to automate phishing attacks
- Can create:
  - Fake login pages
  - Malicious emails
  - Credential harvesting pages

---

## 🧠 Key Takeaway

Humans are often the weakest link in security, making phishing one of the most effective attack vectors.
