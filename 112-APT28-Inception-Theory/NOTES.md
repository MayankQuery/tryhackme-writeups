## 🔹 What is an APT?

APT = Advanced Persistent Threat

Characteristics:
- Highly skilled attackers
- Long-term access
- Targeted attacks
- Often state-sponsored

---

## 🔹 Who is APT28?

APT28 also known as:

- Fancy Bear
- Sofacy
- STRONTIUM
- Sednit

Believed to be linked to Russia.

Targets include:
- Governments
- Military
- Media
- Energy sector
- NATO countries

---

## 🔹 Attack Lifecycle

1. Reconnaissance
2. Initial Access
3. Persistence
4. Privilege Escalation
5. Lateral Movement
6. Collection
7. Exfiltration

---

## 🔹 Initial Access Techniques

- Spear phishing
- Zero-day exploits
- Vulnerable services
- Malicious documents

---

## 🔹 Persistence Techniques

- Registry modification
- Scheduled tasks
- Backdoors
- Web shells

---

## 🔹 Privilege Escalation

- Exploiting vulnerabilities
- Credential dumping
- Token impersonation

---

## 🔹 Lateral Movement

- SMB
- RDP
- Pass-the-Hash
- Remote execution

---

## 🔹 Data Exfiltration

- Encrypted channels
- Cloud storage
- DNS tunneling
- HTTPS traffic

---

## 🧠 Key Takeaway

Understanding real threat actors helps SOC analysts detect advanced attacks faster.
